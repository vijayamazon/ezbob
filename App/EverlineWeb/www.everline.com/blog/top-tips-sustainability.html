Top tips for sustainability

26th July 2013 - Last updated at 12:14

Whether you run your business from home or from an office, these sustainability tips should offer some great green (and money-saving) advice.

Energy smart

Make sure that the last person out of the office turns the lights off. Not only will this cut your electricity bill, it will also reduce the amount of light pollution in the night skies.

If you can, install energy saving light bulbs. Even better, install light-sensor bulbs to ensure that the lights are only on when necessary.

If you have a lot of PCs or printers, enable sleep mode after five minutes. Power strips at turn-off points are also a great long-term investment.

Recycle smart

Recycling shouldn’t be restricted to surplus printouts. Try to reuse (or give away) any old furniture or PCs that you don’t use anymore, and recycle ink and laser jet cartridges.

Print smart

If it’s impractical to go paperless, always print on both sides and use paper with at least 30% recycled content.

If you ever need to print any formal catalogues or publications, request that your printer uses vegetable-based inks.

If none of the above applies to you, ensure that you receive any trade/news/media updates via email format.

Eat smart

Drink tap or filtered water rather than bottled water. If you offer food to your employees or guests, ensure it’s produced locally.

If you employ people in a coworking space, provide reusable cups and dishware in meetings and break areas. If this isn’t possible, paper towels and cups which can be recycled are a good substitute.

Travel smart

If you employ people who commute to work in cars, encourage ride sharing.

If you live in a bike-friendly area, encourage people to cycle to work (which may involve installing showers in the office).

If your business model permits it, working remotely may also be a good way to reduce the transport costs and your office energy consumption.
