Are you ready to put your head in the accounting cloud?

25th September 2014 - Last updated at 12:11

Imagine having your key business data on the go, in real time on your smart phone, and capturing those all important figures faster with less effort.

Armed with accurate, up-to-the-minute information you have the ability to increase productivity, make better decisions, and expand your company. It’s all possible with cloud accounting.

What on earth is cloud computing?

Before you had the internet you bought software with a license and ran it on your computer, you rented movies on video, waited for photos to be developed and bought cassette or vinyl.  Rapidly developing technology has changed the way we do things. Cloud computing means you don’t have to install anything on your computer and you never actually own any software. Instead of buying a product, you’re renting a service.

It’s easy peasy accounting

Keeping track of your accounts in real time is now possible from any computer, phone or tablet that has an internet connection.
It’s simplified how you access your information so that everything you need to know about your business’ financial status is clearly set out on the dashboards, in an easy-to-read format. You can see the complete picture at a glance, including your current bank balance, your accounts receivable and accounts payable balances for the current month and next month, helping manage the cash flow.

Take your accountant to the cloud

You might be thinking you can handle everything yourself, but don’t pick up the phone to tell your accountant it’s over just yet. Cloud accounting actually means better collaboration with your accountant, with less money spent on accountant grunt work, fewer face to face meetings, less physical paperwork, and faster error spotting through their real time remote access, minimising the risk of expensive and time consuming mistakes. Your relationship looks stronger than ever.

Say goodbye to technology worries

When you rent a car, the maintenance, repairs and breakdown assistance are all taken care of by the rental company. You can just jump in your car and drive off. The same is true of cloud accounting.  You get to use it without worrying about installing it, maintaining it, downloading updates or keeping it secure. You don’t need a server or any additional IT investment. Your software takes care of all that. By handing over the operational work to someone else, you’re reducing the risk and unexpected expense associated with IT.

Are you ready to put your head in the accounting cloud?

But is it safe?

Now that we’ve created a pretty compelling argument for cloud accountancy we have to mention the degree, which is rapidly diminishing, of danger when putting your business-critical information in the hands of a third party. You wouldn’t buy a car from a dodgy dealer, and you should apply the same level of caution when choosing cloud accountancy software.

Rest assured, if your accountancy software and third party is reliable, they will do a better job of looking after your data than you could by yourself.

The future is cloudy

With cloud accountancy you’ll no longer have to wait until the year end to know how your business performed throughout that year. Now you can view all your accounting information as and when you want it, wherever you want it. Real-time, online accounting makes everything faster and simpler.

There are plenty of free cloud accounting trials out there, but we like www.freeagent.co.uk, www.kashflow.com and www.xero.com. Give them a go today or tell us which cloud accounting software you use.
