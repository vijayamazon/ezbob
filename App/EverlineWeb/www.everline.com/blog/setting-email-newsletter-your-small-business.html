Setting up an email newsletter for your small business

6th February 2014 - Last updated at 14:51

Your mailing list

The first thing to do is compile your marketing mailing list. There are strict data protection rules about who you can send e-newsletters to –for a start you need to ensure you have obtained the requisite permission from any potential recipients.  You should seek legal advice to ensure your business complies with all regulations, including data protection legislation, that could apply to your marketing or communication campaigns.

Your content

Email newsletters can live or die on their content. Don't just promote yourself – you may want to include useful, entertaining and relevant content that isn't available anywhere else and that meets a customer need (eg answering common questions).

A good subject line could encourage more people to open the newsletter – try interesting questions or flagging an exclusive sale or discount for subscribers. Give recipients a good reason to read.

You must include a clear link in every email to enable recipients to unsubscribe from  your mailing lists.  As highlighted above, you should seek legal advice about this issue, to ensure you comply with all relevant regulations.

Finally, you may want to feature links that make it easy for people to share your newsletter with their friends, and to do business with you – include your full contact details and links to your website and social media profiles.

Design and technical side

It's not likely that you'll have your own in-house tech team to design your newsletter. Fortunately there are plenty of reputable email marketing companies which can handle it all for you – most will offer:

  • fully designed templates;
  • list management (including unsubscribes);
  • sign-up form 'widgets' for your website and social media;
  • reports on who read and who clicked.
