Improving your press coverage without hiring a PR team

4th March 2014 - Last updated at 11:03

SMEs may be able to improve awareness of their business, by crafting compelling press releases, targeting them to the right people and considering the following steps.

  1. Doing your research

    Consider approaching journalists and publications (or websites) that are a good fit for your business. This could include small and niche – such asyour local newspaper or industry interest sites before the 'big boy' nationals. A personalised pitch, showing you've read the writer's previous work, might catch someone's ear – a generic 'Dear Sir or Madam' may end up straight in the bin.

  2. Thinking of the audience

    Don't just stuff your press releases with dull, dry facts or flowery sales copy. Remember – the writer is not there to promote you, but to provide eye-catching, readable articles for their readers. If they can't make your story sound interesting, why should they cover it? Consider whether to provide (or offer to send) digital, high-resolution photographs to go with your story – images can help to catch the eye.

  3. Getting to the point

    Journalists receive a deluge of press releases every day, and may not have time to read each one in detail. You might be able to make life easy for them by providing all the key facts up-front in your release – the who, what, when, where and why – and spelling out why the story will appeal to their audience.

  4. Telling a story

    The aim of press coverage is often to raise awareness of a business – not necessarily to directly sell its products. Consider the engaging 'human interest' angle. A good example could be a story on how your business has benefited the local community. You might also be able to add colour with quotes from key people, such as a local councillor.

  5. Making yourself available

    Finally, journalists are often racing against time to file copy. If you can fit in with their time-pressured way of working – making yourself easy to contact and responding to queries quickly – it could be the beginning of a beautiful friendship.
