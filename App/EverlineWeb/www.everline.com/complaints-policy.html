Complaints policy

Our customers are at the heart of our business and we’re committed to giving them the highest quality service we can. So if you’re not happy with our service, let us know so we can put things right.

How to contact us to make a complaint

By email: customercomplaints@everline.com

By telephone: 0800 840 7750 (lines are open between 7:30am and 7:00pm, Monday-Friday. If you need to contact us outside of these hours, please leave a message and we will call you back as soon as possible). Calls are charged at an average of 4p per minute for landline customers. Calls from mobile phones may be higher).

How long will it take?

We strive to acknowledge all customer complaints within 48 hours of receiving them and we aim to resolve them within 7 days.

If we haven’t been able to resolve your complaint within 7 days, we’ll be in touch to let you know:

  • Why we haven’t resolved it
  • Who is dealing with it
  • When we’ll contact you again

If a complaint is particularly complex, it may take longer to resolve.

We’ll try our best to resolve your complaint as quickly as possible and we’ll send you our final response within 8 weeks.

If we can’t respond in this time, we’ll contact you to explain the delay and give you an indication of when to expect our response.

If you’re unhappy with the progress of your complaint at this point, in certain circumstances you can refer it to the Financial Ombudsman Service. Please refer to the Financial Ombudsman Service section below to find out which types of customer can refer a complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

Where to go if you’re still not happy?

If you’re unhappy with our final response, in certain circumstances you will be able to refer your complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS).

Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)

You may be able to contact the FOS if you are one of the following types of business customer at the time that the complaint is referred to us by you:

  • A sole trader or other unincorporated organisation (unless the organisation is made up entirely of bodies corporate) which employs fewer than 10 persons and has a turnover or annual balance sheet that does not exceed 2 million euros (or a sterling equivalent); or
  • A partnership (with 3 or less partners which are not bodies corporate) which employs fewer than 10 persons and has a turnover or annual balance sheet that does not exceed 2 million euros (or a sterling equivalent).

If you want the FOS to consider your complaint, you must send your complaint to them within 6 months of the date of our final response. Their contact details are:
