  • Our top 5 FAQs

    • How does Everline work?

      We took a fresh look at business loans, and developed our service to make everything simple and transparent. You can use our online sliders to select the amount you need and the period you need it for. You’ll be able to see all the costs upfront and know exactly what you’ll repay.

      Simple to arrange – with all costs shown up front

    • What do I need to apply for an Everline loan?

      Your business needs to be a limited company or limited liability partnership. Each director or partner of the business needs to be over 18, and a UK resident. Your business also needs to have a business debit card attached to the main business bank account.

      Your business will also need to have been operating and actively trading for at least 18 months in the UK, submitted at least one set of accounts to Companies House, and have sufficient revenue to cover the weekly repayments.

      Please don't apply for a loan with us if you have any concerns about your business’ ability to meet these requirements.

      The main applicant will also need to provide a personal guarantee for the business loan and give us details of their personal debit card. We may use it to collect the amount owed if the business fails to pay.

      To give your business the best chance of being accepted, make sure you give us as much information as possible and ensure it is accurate when you apply. If you’re an existing Everline customer, you can keep your details up to date by logging into the ‘my account’ area of our site.

    • How much can my business borrow?

      You can apply for £3,000-50,000 on the first application.

      Simply use the sliders on the Get a Quote page to select the business loan size and length (1-52 weeks). We’ll show the total cost of the loan and the weekly repayments.

       At the outset you can extend your loan length to reduce your weekly repayment and repay early at any time to save as you only pay interest for the number of weeks that you borrow the money. As you are paying back an equal portion of the original loan amount each week, the longer the initial term the less the weekly repayment. If you don’t repay early, a longer term will mean your interest cost will be higher.

    • What does an Everline business loan cost?

      Our interest rate is fixed at 0.5% per week and this equates to an interest cost of 26% for a 52 week loan. We also charge an arrangement fee of 1.5% of the loan amount (the arrangement fee does not attract interest).

      Interest and the loan fee are added to the principal amount of the loan at the outset, which is then divided by the number of weeks during your chosen loan term in order to establish your equal weekly repayments.

      If the business’s circumstances change and the loan can be repaid earlier than expected, you may be able to save money. The business can repay early in full or in part at any time at no extra charge, and interest will only be charged for the number of weeks that you borrow the money.

    • How do I repay?

      Just ensure the required cash is in the main business bank account (or personal bank account for of sole traders) on the day the weekly repayment is due.

      Repayments are made by continuous payment authority using your business debit card (or personal debit card in the case of a sole trader), starting on the seventh day after we send the cash, and then weekly for the rest of the term.

      Please make sure you read the ‘how we collect’ payment document. It explains how we collect weekly repayments from your card on each promise date and if necessary after that date if the amount is still outstanding. It also explains your rights to cancel card payments.

      If we can’t collect the amount you owe at first, we may make further attempts to collect your repayment using the debit card we have on file for you.

      The business can repay early in full or in part at any time at no extra charge, and interest will only be charged for the weeks when you borrow the money.

      To make early repayments, please call us on 0203 371 0322

      For security reasons, you should never send any details about your business or personal bank account or debit card details to us via email or live chat.
