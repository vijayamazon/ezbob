  • How does Everline work?

  • What do I need to apply for an Everline loan?

    Your business needs to be a limited company or limited liability partnership. Each director or partner of the business needs to be over 18, and a UK resident. Your business also needs to have a business debit card attached to the main business bank account.

    Your business will also need to have been operating and actively trading for at least 18 months in the UK, submitted at least one set of accounts to Companies House, and have sufficient revenue to cover the weekly repayments.

    Please don't apply for a loan with us if you have any concerns about your business’ ability to meet these requirements.

    The main applicant will also need to provide a personal guarantee for the business loan and give us details of their personal debit card. We may use it to collect the amount owed if the business fails to pay.

    To give your business the best chance of being accepted, make sure you give us as much information as possible and ensure it is accurate when you apply. If you’re an existing Everline customer, you can keep your details up to date by logging into the ‘my account’ area of our site.

  • How much can my business borrow?

    You can apply for £3,000-50,000 on the first application.

    Simply use the sliders on the Get a Quote page to select the business loan size and length (1-52 weeks). We’ll show the total cost of the loan and the weekly repayments.

     At the outset you can extend your loan length to reduce your weekly repayment and repay early at any time to save as you only pay interest for the number of weeks that you borrow the money. As you are paying back an equal portion of the original loan amount each week, the longer the initial term the less the weekly repayment. If you don’t repay early, a longer term will mean your interest cost will be higher.

  • What does an Everline business loan cost?

    Our interest rate is fixed at 0.5% per week and this equates to an interest cost of 26% for a 52 week loan. We also charge an arrangement fee of 1.5% of the loan amount (the arrangement fee does not attract interest).

    Interest and the loan fee are added to the principal amount of the loan at the outset, which is then divided by the number of weeks during your chosen loan term in order to establish your equal weekly repayments.

    If the business’s circumstances change and the loan can be repaid earlier than expected, you may be able to save money. The business can repay early in full or in part at any time at no extra charge, and interest will only be charged for the number of weeks that you borrow the money.

    If we can’t collect the amount you owe at first, we may make further attempts to collect your repayment using the debit card we have on file for you.

  • How does Everline collect repayments?

    Using the card details you give us when you apply (or when you add a card), we use continuous payment authority (CPA) to collect weekly repayments on the agreed promise dates. Your promise dates are explained in the pre-contract information and your credit agreement. You need to agree to CPA to get an Everline loan.
    You can cancel your CPA anytime by writing “Cancel Continuous Payment Authority” in an email to customercare@everline.com, or by contacting your bank directly. Remember, if you do this, you must arrange for another way to pay us.
    For details of other ways to pay us please see Can I repay an Everline Loan by bank transfer? or contact 0800 840 7750

  • What happens if Everline can’t collect the full amount of a weekly repayment?

    On the weekly promise date:
    We try to collect your weekly repayment amount from your card from 5am on each weekly promise date. If we are not able to collect the full amount at this time, we will try again at 5pm on the same day. We will also send you an email and an SMS to inform you that the collection attempt failed. See the “Will Everline charge me for missing repayments?” section below for information on the late payment fees you may be charged.
    On the second day after that promise date:
    Unless you have paid the full weekly repayment due using an alternative payment method, we will try to collect the repayment amount again on the second day after your promise date at 5am and then, if that attempt is unsuccessful, at 5pm. We will send you an email and SMS to inform you that the collection attempt failed. Again, see the “Will Everline charge me for missing repayments?” section below for information on late payment fees.
    On the next weekly promise date:
    If a weekly repayment remains unpaid on the next promise date, we will attempt to collect the amount due as follows, using CPA:
    Firstly, we will try to collect the amount due on that promise date.
    If we are successful in collecting that amount, we will try to collect outstanding repayments from previous weeks, excluding any fees payable.
    If we are successful in collecting all weekly repayment amounts outstanding and due, we will try to collect the amount of any late payment fees along with any other fees outstanding.
    On subsequent promise dates:
    If any amount remains outstanding, we will continue to try to collect that amount as set out above on each subsequent promise date unless we make alternative arrangements with you.
    If you experience financial difficulties, you should contact us immediately on 0800 840 7750 to discuss your options and we will work with you to help find the most suitable way forward.
    After 8 missed payments:
    If you are 8 or more payments in arrears and no repayment method has been agreed and you have not been identified as in hardship or vulnerable then the loan can be rolled up with the whole balance due and will be classed as in default. This could have a negative impact on your credit rating.

  • Will Everline charge me for missing repayments?

    If we are unable to collect the full amount of the weekly payment by 5pm on the promise date, we will add a late payment fee of £5 to your account.
    If we are still unable to collect the full amount of the weekly payment by 5pm on the second day after the promise date, we will add another late payment fee of £5 to your account.
    Your bank may also charge you for refusing our payment requests.
    To avoid a late payment fee, you need to ensure you either have enough money in your account or that you pay using an alternative payment method before 5pm on your promise date.

  • Why can’t I login to My account?

    This could be for a couple of reasons. Firstly the password field is case sensitive so if you used lowercase and capital letters, you need to make sure you retype the password exactly the same when logging in. You can’t cut and paste your password from another document for security reasons, so make sure you type it in.
    Secondly, make sure you’re entering the email address you registered with. For example, if you used a personal email address, you won’t be able to use your work email to login, unless you change it in your account page first. The email field is also case sensitive, so if you used any capital letters when you first entered it, you’ll need to type it the same way every time you login.

  • I can’t remember my password. What should I do?

    Don’t worry, you just need to get a new one at the “my account” page. We’ll email a randomly generated password to your registered email address. You can’t cut and paste the new password from the email, you’ll have to type it in as normal then memorize it or change it to a new one once you’ve logged in.

  • I haven’t received my email during application. What should I do?

    It’s probably because your email provider has mistaken our confirmation email as spam (unsolicited junk email). First, check your spam or junk folder to see if it’s in there. If so, it’s a good idea to then make our email address customercare@everline.com an accepted address or simply add it to your contacts. That will make sure your email provider knows messages from us are safe.

  • I haven't received my PIN via text. What should I do?

    Give it 30 minutes to be sure. If you still haven’t received anything, check you entered the correct mobile number when applying and, if need be, start again with a fresh application. Make sure you enter the number of the mobile phone you have with you.
